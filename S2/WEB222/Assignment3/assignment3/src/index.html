Taiwanese music

Mandarin Music Center

Due to various historical and political reasons, Taiwan is the freest Chinese pop music center, and its influence ranges from Japan to Singapore, all East Asia, and global Chinese culture. Taiwan’s music industry has also been continuously developing and has many talented musicians. In addition, Taiwan’s geographical location has made it a cultural exchange center in East Asia. In the Golden Melody Awards in recent years, you can see a variety of music creations, using Hakka, Taiwanese, aboriginal languages, and various types of creations.

Garbage truck music

Taiwan has a unique garbage truck culture where the trucks play music. The “garbage doesn’t touch the ground” policy requires residents to bring trash directly out of their homes to the trucks, resulting in more hygienic streets in Taiwan’s sweaty climate. The government has implemented what is commonly known in Taiwan as a “trash doesn’t touch the ground” system - and to newcomers, this can be mind-boggling. Residents gather on the streets with their bags of rubbish and have to throw them into the trucks themselves.

Pop singer

Teresa Teng (鄧麗君)

Teresa Teng was a Taiwanese singer and cultural icon who rose to fame during the 1970s and became well-known in many Asian countries. She was known for her folk songs and romantic ballads, and her image, music and popularity made her one of the greatest cultural icons in Chinese-speaking world. She recorded songs not only in Mandarin but also in Taiwanese Hokkien, Cantonese, Japanese, Indonesian and English. Her songs are still popular today, there is a good chance that some of these songs will be heard during the Lunar New Year.

Coco Lee (李玟)

Coco Lee is a Hong Kong-born American singer. Lee's career began in Hong Kong and then expanded to Taiwan. As a Chinese American, Lee is the first and only person of Chinese descent to perform at the Oscars; she performed the Best Original Song nominated, "A Love Before Time" from the movie Crouching Tiger, Hidden Dragon. She also sang the Mandarin version of the theme song “Reflection” from 1998 Disney movie Mulan.

Jolin Tsai (蔡依林)

Jolin Tsai is a Taiwanese singer, songwriter, dancer, actress, and businesswoman. Tsai began her singing career by winning the champion at an MTV singing competition at the age of 18. Her debut album, released in 1999 and titled 1019, was a huge success, selling over 400,000 copies in Taiwan alone. Tsai is one of the best-selling artists in Asia, having sold more than 25 million records worldwide. She is queen of C-POP. There are many international cooperations, It was the third anniversary theme song of the mobile game, PUBG Mobile. Recently, she sang on the very famous Japan Youtube “THE FIRST TAKE” in She presents her hit song, "Womxnly (玫瑰少年)," which also won Song of the Year at the 30th Golden Melody Awards, is a song supporting LQBTQ+ community.

Chthonic (閃靈)

Chthonic is a Taiwanese heavy metal band, formed in 1995 in Taipei. They have been called "the Black Sabbath of Asia." The group incorporates influences from traditional Taiwanese music including the classical Chinese instrument erhu. The band is also known for its advocacy of Taiwanese independence, human rights, and Taiwanese localization movement. The band’s name is derived from the Greek word “khthonios,” which means “underworld” or “subterranean” and is pronounced as “thonic”. The lead vocalist of the band, Freddy Lim, is also a member of the Legislative Congress in Taiwan.
